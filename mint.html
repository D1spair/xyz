Introduction

The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the Soviet Union and the United States and their respective allies, the Eastern Bloc and the Western Bloc, after World War II. The period is generally considered to span from 1947, the year the Truman Doctrine was announced, to 1991, the year the Soviet Union collapsed.

Key Events

  • 1947: Truman Doctrine
  • 1948-1949: Berlin Blockade
  • 1950-1953: Korean War
  • 1961: Construction of the Berlin Wall
  • 1962: Cuban Missile Crisis
  • 1979-1989: Soviet-Afghan War
  • 1989: Fall of the Berlin Wall
  • 1991: Dissolution of the Soviet Union

Notable Figures

  • Harry S. Truman
  • Joseph Stalin
  • John F. Kennedy
  • Nikita Khrushchev
  • Ronald Reagan
  • Mikhail Gorbachev

Timeline

1947

Truman Doctrine announced, beginning the policy of containment against Soviet expansion.

1948-1949

Berlin Blockade and Airlift: The Soviet Union blocked Western Allies' railway and road access to the sectors of Berlin under Western control.

1962

Cuban Missile Crisis: A 13-day confrontation between the United States and the Soviet Union initiated by American ballistic missile deployment in Italy and Turkey with consequent Soviet ballistic missile deployment in Cuba.

1989

Fall of the Berlin Wall: The Berlin Wall, a symbol of the Cold War, was opened, leading to the reunification of Germany.

1991

Dissolution of the Soviet Union: The USSR was officially dissolved, ending the Cold War.
